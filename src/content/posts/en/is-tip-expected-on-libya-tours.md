<p><strong>Nope.</strong> Tipping is always optional, and some tour operators like ourselves don't accept tips.</p>

<p>There is no rigid percentage theatre here like some US restaurant cultures. Gratitude is welcome in spirit, but you should never feel pressured. Ask your operator what their policy is before you travel. IntoLibya does not accept tips.</p>

<h2>What IntoLibya's policy means for guests</h2>

<p>Your tour price already covers the licensed plan: sponsorship support, guides, transport, and on ground logistics. You do not need to budget tip envelopes to make the week work. Say thank you with words, photos you are allowed to take, and a kind review if the trip earned it.</p>

<p>Other operators may set different customs. Always ask before you travel so you are not guessing at the airport curb.</p>

<h2>What not to do</h2>

<p>Do not tip as a bribe for rule breaking. Do not tip children at ruins in ways that encourage begging patterns. Do not feel awkward for keeping your wallet closed when the operator policy says tips are not accepted. Clear gratitude beats confusing cash.</p>

<h2>If you still want to say thanks</h2>

<p>A sincere thank you to your guide at the end of the trip goes a long way. Share what moment mattered. If something went wrong, talk to IntoLibya directly through TourBuilder rather than trying to solve it with money.</p>

<p>Bottom line: tips are optional, never expected with us, and never a requirement for a great Libya week.</p>
