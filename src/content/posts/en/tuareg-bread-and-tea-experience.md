<h2>How the tea ritual usually unfolds</h2>

<p>Tea is prepared in rounds with attention to sweetness and foam. Pouring from height is part craft, part theatre, part practical aeration. You watch, wait, sip, and talk. Rushing the ritual misses the point. The desert teaches that haste is sometimes a city disease.</p>

<p>Bread prepared in traditional style arrives as the grounding companion: simple, fragrant, meant for sharing. Together they turn a stop into a ceremony.</p>

<h2>Respect and participation</h2>

<p>Follow host cues. Accept what is offered unless dietary limits were arranged earlier. Ask before photos of people at work. If you are invited to pour or help, treat it as honor, not content for mockery.</p>

<p>Dress modestly. Keep voices warm. Curiosity beats performance.</p>
