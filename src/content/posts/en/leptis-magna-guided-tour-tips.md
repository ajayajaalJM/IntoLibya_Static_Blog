<p>Start with a wide orientation so your brain maps scale. Move through monumental civic spaces while attention is fresh. Save long transverse walks for moments when shade and breeze help. Let the theatre and coastal edges become emotional peaks rather than early burnout.</p>

<h2>Heat, water, and shoes</h2>

<p>Stone radiates. Hats and sunscreen are not optional fashion. Carry more water than pride suggests. Wear shoes with real soles; ancient paving does not care about your aesthetics.</p>

<p>Midday light flattens relief carving. Morning and late day flatter photographers. If your schedule forces noon, lean into details and shade compositions instead of chasing perfect sky drama.</p>
