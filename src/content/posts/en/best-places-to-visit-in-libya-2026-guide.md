<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><a href="https://intolibya.com/leptis-magna/">Leptis Magna</a></h2>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><a href="/en/destination/leptis-magna">Leptis Magna</a> is widely recognized as one of the best preserved Roman cities in the world. Located along the Mediterranean coast east of <a href="/en/destination/tripoli">Tripoli</a>, it was expanded during the reign of Emperor Septimius Severus, who was born in the region.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">When visiting with IntoLibya, guests explore the amphitheatre overlooking the sea, the grand forum, public baths, markets, and triumphal arches. Because the site receives relatively few visitors, the experience feels calm and immersive.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Furthermore, IntoLibya provides knowledgeable local guides who explain the historical significance of each structure. As a result, the ruins become more than stone remains. They become part of a larger story about Libya’s place in the Roman world.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">For history enthusiasts, <a href="/en/destination/leptis-magna">Leptis Magna</a> is essential.</p>

<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><a href="https://intolibya.com/ghadames/">Ghadames</a></h2>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><a href="/en/destination/ghadames">Ghadames</a> is often called the Pearl of the Desert. Situated near Libya’s borders with Algeria and Tunisia, this ancient oasis town is known for its unique architecture and Amazigh heritage.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The old town is built from mud brick and lime. Covered alleyways protect residents from the desert sun, while rooftop pathways allow movement above the streets. Because of its architectural and cultural importance, <a href="/en/destination/ghadames">Ghadames</a> is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">IntoLibya includes guided exploration of the old city, allowing travelers to understand how desert communities adapted to extreme climates. In addition, guests experience the surrounding palm groves and quiet desert landscape, which create a peaceful and memorable atmosphere.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><a href="https://intolibya.com/acacus-mountains/">Acacus Mountains</a></h2>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The <a href="/en/destination/acacus-mountains">Acacus Mountains</a> rise dramatically from the desert floor in southwestern Libya. Over thousands of years, wind and sand have shaped the rock formations into arches, towers, and narrow valleys.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">More importantly, the region contains prehistoric rock art that dates back over ten thousand years. These paintings depict animals and daily life from a time when the Sahara was green and fertile.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Although the <a href="/en/destination/acacus-mountains">Acacus</a> region is remote, IntoLibya arranges structured desert expeditions using experienced drivers and local guides. Guests travel in four wheel drive vehicles and camp under the stars with proper logistical support. Therefore, travelers can experience the desert safely and comfortably.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Ubari Sand Sea</h2>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The Ubari Sand Sea is one of the most impressive desert landscapes in North Africa. Massive golden dunes stretch across the horizon, creating a scene that changes color throughout the day.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">During an IntoLibya Sahara extension, guests explore the dunes by four wheel drive vehicle. In addition, they visit desert lakes such as <a href="/en/destination/gaberoun">Gaberoun</a> and <a href="/en/destination/um-el-ma">Um el Ma</a>, which appear unexpectedly between the dunes.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">At sunset, the sand reflects warm tones of gold and orange. Later, the night sky fills with stars. Because the desert is far from city lights, the view is exceptionally clear.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><a href="https://intolibya.com/sabratha/">Sabratha</a></h2>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><a href="/en/destination/sabratha">Sabratha</a> is another important Roman site located west of <a href="/en/destination/tripoli">Tripoli</a>. Although smaller than Leptis Magna, it remains historically significant.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The Roman theatre at <a href="/en/destination/sabratha">Sabratha</a> stands near the Mediterranean Sea and is remarkably well preserved. In addition, the site includes temples, columns, and mosaics that highlight the prosperity of ancient Libya.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">IntoLibya includes Sabratha as part of its coastal heritage exploration, ensuring a balanced experience between major and lesser known Roman sites.</p>
