<h2>Photography strategy across lakes</h2>

<p>Each stop offers different shore shapes and dune backdrops. Resist identical photos at every waterline. Change elevation, include palms as scale, and capture people small against sand to show true size.</p>

<p>Midday glare is real. Use it for graphic contrast or wait for kinder angles when the schedule allows. Your guide knows which lake loves which light.</p>

<h2>Energy management</h2>

<p>Three stops sound light until soft sand and sun stack up. Eat, drink, and accept that not every lake needs a full swim. Sometimes the wisest traveler is the one who sits, looks, and saves legs for the next ridge.</p>
